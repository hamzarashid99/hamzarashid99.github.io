About Me

I am a research assistant at Center for Urban, Informatics, Technology and Policy (CITY) at Lahore University of Management Sciences (LUMS). I graduated from LUMS as an Electrical Engineer in May 2023. My current work revolves around urban disaster management and GIS-based spatial and temporal analysis. My interests lie in developing models for sustainable development that would help in mitigating global climatic changes occuring throughout the world. I am also interested in using deep learning models in biomedical applications such as analyzing patterns in brain development and neural connections of the body.

My Projects

Air quality estimation and sensor recommendation system

In my senior year of undergraduate studies, I utilized a Gaussian Processes Regression (GPR) model to develop a spatio-temporal PM 2.5 concentration prediction system. To enhance the accuracy of the predictions, I integrated a PM 2.5 sensor placement recommendation system. This system recommends optimal locations for additional sensors based on prediction uncertainties, thereby leading to more precise results.

Urban Flood Prediction

The objective of this project is to generate a flood susceptibility map for flood-vulnerable areas in Lahore, boasting a spatial resolution of 30 meters. I am leveraging Sentinel 1 Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) and Sentinel 2 multi-band imagery, complemented by the relevant hydrological features specific to Lahore. Given the limited availability of reliable ground-truth data, I have adopted a weakly supervised learning approach to address this challenge. Furthermore, by integrating real-time rainfall data with global rain prediction models, I aim to establish a warning system that provides timely alerts to local authorities in areas susceptible to flooding.

Reinforcement Learning using Q-Learning

We developed a prototype for an educational application aimed at teaching children fundamental mathematics. In this prototype, correctly answering math problems helps users capture a simulated thief in a confined environment. Conversely, incorrect answers increase the distance between the player and the thief, leveraging a reward-based Q-Learning game mechanism. The game dynamically introduces varying levels of difficulty by integrating obstacles and rewards, the latter being contingent on the probability of facing tougher questions. The model was exhaustively trained within a 10x10 grid setup, featuring two pursuers and one evader.

RF Vital Sign Sensing

In this research, we devised a system that harnesses RFID tags to gauge total body movement power, facilitating the classification of an individual's activity intensity. The primary objectives of capturing total body movement power was its potential to predict a person's respiration rate. Data was gathered from 20 participants across three distinct activity states: running, walking, and resting. For the model training, we employed a bi-directional LSTM to predict the respiration rate.

Laser Engraver

In this course project, we designed and built a prototype of a laser engraver capable of translating binary-format images into detailed engravings on cardboard sheets. The laser's activation sequences and the stepper motors' control were programmed in assembly language, utilizing a custom-designed microcontroller.
